## About

> The U.S. [**Department of Energy**](https://www.energy.gov/)’s Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory (PPPL) is mastering the art of using [**plasma**](https://youtu.be/M8cSQltH6TU?si=DYmMMtcfpa_VJE-P) — the fourth state of matter — to solve some of the world’s toughest science and technology challenges. That’s why public and private researchers worldwide look to PPPL for guidance on plasma science and related engineering challenges.

## Mission Areas

[*Source*](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas)

### [**Civil Space Flight→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/civil-space-flight)

Delivering critical spaceflight hardware to NASA, international partners, and other U.S. government civilian agencies

### [**Cyber Operations→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/cyber-operations)

Creating disruptive cyber capabilities to ensure U.S. advantage

### [**Global Health→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/global-health)

Eliminating naturally occurring, deliberate, and accidental health threats through revolutionary science and engineering

### [**Homeland Defense→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/homeland-defense)

Developing innovative and effective solutions to defend the U.S. homeland and its critical infrastructure from increasingly diverse and sophisticated adversary threats

### [**National Security Analysis→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/national-security-analysis)

Combining innovative technical knowledge and operational analysis to create emerging capability solutions to national security challenges

### [**National Security Space→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/national-security-space)

Enabling mission partners at all levels of government to realize critically important missions for national security

### [**Precision Strike→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/precision-strike)

Delivering critical precision strike contributions, addressing tactical aircraft, kinetic and non-kinetic weapon systems, and end-to-end capability development and demonstration

### [**Research and Exploratory Development→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/research-and-exploratory-development)

Conducting pioneering research targeting game-changing breakthroughs to benefit national security missions

### [**Sea Control→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/sea-control)

Supporting U.S. Navy and joint service missions and delivering essential capabilities to project military power on, under, and above the seas

### [**Space Formulation→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/space-formulation)

Leveraging the expertise of world-class scientists and engineers to provide NASA and our partners with innovative solutions to the critical challenges they face in the space domain

### [**Special Operations→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/special-operations)

Creating disruptive capabilities that enable special intelligence operations to rapidly characterize and defeat asymmetric threats

[**Learn more about Special Operations**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/special-operations)

### [**Strategic Deterrence→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/strategic-deterrence)

Transforming strategic systems from their legacy nuclear mission to a broad set of responses to current and future national security challenges

### [**Theater Defense→**](https://www.jhuapl.edu/work/mission-areas/theater-defense)

Creating innovative and effective system solutions to defend forward-deployed U.S. forces, allies, and partners across the globe from the threat of air and missile attack
